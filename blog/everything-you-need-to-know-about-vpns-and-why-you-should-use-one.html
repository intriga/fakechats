Everything You Need to Know About VPNs and Why You Should Use One

by fakedetail217 March 18, 2025 0

Know About VPNs – Online privacy and security are a top priority for the majority of people. However, our details are very vulnerable whenever we use the internet. These details are exposed when we do online banking or even when we use social media. In this situation, a virtual private network, or commonly known VPN, comes to the rescue. The internet world is limitless and risky for your data. In such a circumstance, a VPN can work as a safety net to keep your details safe. 

What is a VPN?

A VPN is a confidential way to use the internet. It passes your information and online traffic through a distant server and encrypts it. It is as if you are transferring your data via a safe, protected truck rather than a public and unsafe route. This procedure becomes a protective barrier to your IP address (your device’s unique identifier online) and puts the VPN server’s IP address in its place. Because of this, it becomes far more difficult for everyone, including the following, to follow your online activities or determine where you are: 

  1. Online fraudsters
  2. Cyber criminals
  3. Marketing agencies
  4. Internet service providers

How Does a VPN Work?

An encrypted connection and routing system are its main features. We have mentioned below its complete mechanism: 

1. Encryption

When you connect to a VPN, it makes a secure link between your computer and the VPN server. This encryption encodes your data with complicated algorithms so that anybody who acquires a hold of it can’t read it.

2. Tunneling

A secure “tunnel” secures your data as it makes its way to the VPN server. No one else can access your data and private information because this tunnel separates it from the rest of the internet.

3. IP address marking 

When your data gets to the VPN server, it is given the IP address of the server. Sites and services you visit see the IP address of the VPN server instead of your own when you connect to them. This successfully hides where you are and who you are.

4. Decryption

When data comes back to you from a website or service, it goes through the same steps again, but this time in the opposite order. The data is encrypted by the VPN server and sent through the tunnel. When it gets to your device, it decrypts the data.

Why should you use a VPN?

Using a VPN has many advantages besides just keeping your information safe. Here are some strong reasons why you should use a VPN every time you go online:

1. Enhanced Security on Public Wi-Fi

Public Wi-Fis is a simple and easy way to do various activities online. However, they are often unsecured. There’s a good chance that anyone using the same Wi-Fi could get access to what you’re doing online and steal private data like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal emails. A VPN protects your link, which makes it much harder for hackers to steal your information, even on networks that are known to be weak.

2. Bypass Geo-Restrictions and Censorship

You can’t use all video streaming services, websites, or internet material if you live in certain areas. With a VPN, you can connect to a computer in a different country and appear to be in a different place. This lets you see material that might not be available in your geographical region. In the same way, a VPN can help you get around countries that strictly block some online material countries with strict internet censorship and give you access to a free and open internet.

3. Protect Your Privacy from ISPs and Advertisers

Many websites and marketers use cookies and other tracking tools to keep an eye on what you do online, and so can your internet service provider. A lot of the time, this information is sold to third parties or used to make focused advertising profiles. It is much harder for ISPs and ads to keep track of what you do online when you use a VPN. This is because it hides your IP address and encrypts your data.

4. Safe online banking and shopping

It is very important to be safe when doing banking deals online. By securing your link, a VPN adds an extra layer of security. This makes it less likely that hackers will steal your private financial information.

5. Improved Online Gaming

Some VPNs can even make your online gaming experience better. You might have lower lag (ping) and a more stable link if you join a site that is closer to the game server. DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) attacks can also be stopped by a VPN. These attacks are sometimes used by malicious players to disrupt your game.

How to Choose the Right VPN?

There are many different VPN providers available today. Therefore, choosing the best one as per your specific need can be a daunting task. Therefore, we have put down a few key aspects that you should consider when you finalize a VPN for yourself: 

  • Security

The main purpose of a VPN is to ensure your security when you use the internet. You might be using the internet for the following purposes: 

  • Accessing news, research articles, tutorials, how-to guides, 
  • Streaming movies, TV shows, music, podcasts, and playing online games.
  • Participating in online courses, 
  • Shopping
  • Working remotely or collaborating with colleagues
  • Banking
  • Using Social Networking Channels

In the above circumstances, your security is very vulnerable. Therefore, you should choose a VPN that employs strong encryption standards and provides functionalities like kill switches and DNS leak safeguards. (which will automatically disconnect you from the internet if the VPN drops) and DNS leak protection.

  • Privacy 

Your privacy can be compromised because of 

  • Private VPN usage
  • Working on sensitive company data
  • Internet Service Provider (ISP) monitoring
  • Online government and non-government surveillance 

In such circumstances, a VPN can become an extra layer of protection if the provider has a strict no-logs policy. It should ensure that your online actions aren’t tracked or stored, and the provider’s location matters due to differing data retention laws across countries.

  • Speed

VPNs can sometimes slow down your internet speed due to the following reasons: 

  1. Encryption process
  2. Server Load
  3. Distance of the server
  4. Bandwidth Restrictions
  5. VPN Protocols

Therefore, considering all the above issues, you should look for providers that offer fast and reliable servers in various locations.

  • Easy to Use

The VPN can be considered easy to use when it can do the following: 

  1. Simple to install and set up
  2. Quickly connect and change settings
  3. Compatibility with various devices and operating systems

  • Price

VPN prices vary, with monthly, yearly, and multi-year subscription options. You should consider a VPN plan that best suits your needs or a VPN server provider that tailors the plans to your needs.

  • Customer Support

Reliable client service is essential in case you encounter any technical issues, bill queries, or connection problems or require feature support. 

Potential Drawbacks of Using a VPN

While there are many advantages to VPNs, they also face a few challenges:

  • Reduced Speed

As discussed earlier, the speed might get compromised because of various factors. A premium VPN provider can address these issues easily.

  • Cost

Many VPNs are free, but they also come with limitations, and they may even compromise your privacy. Paid VPNs can provide good security, better performance, and enhanced features.

  • Complexity

There is not much of a technical knowledge requirement, but many VPNs can be difficult to understand. 

  • VPN Blocks

 Some services try to block VPN usage, such as: 

  1. Streaming Platforms like Netflix
  2. Music Streaming Services, namely Spotify
  3. News platforms, e.g, BBC News
  4. Sports streaming services such as DAZN.
