How to Know that an Online Casino is Not Fake?  

by fakedetail217 August 09, 2024 0

Fake casinos are a major issue in the Philippines. PAGCOR, the country’s licensing authority, warned in September 2023 about gambling sites misusing their logos without proper licenses. Authorities estimate that 250 to 300 offshore casinos are operating illegally. In a shocking turn, Bamban town Mayor Alice Guo became a fugitive amid serious espionage charges related to running fraudulent gambling sites. Guo went into hiding after an arrest warrant was issued, raising further suspicion of her involvement. These scam sites can steal your money, so read on for tips on how to spot fake online casinos and protect yourself.

Why is it Important to Choose a Legal Casino? 

Any casino regulated by governmental bodies is legal. In this case, PAGCOR (Philippine Amusement and Gaming Corporation). For foreign gambling, a license by a recognized jurisdiction like MGA or UKGC is sufficient. A gambling permit shows the operator complies with strict security protocols regarding player and fund protection. Not only this, but the license guarantees the games are fair. With this, players have a fair chance of winning. 

Funds are safe and secure in legal casinos. This includes deposits, winnings, and payouts. When you run into problems, online casino real money in the Philippines has dispute resolution mechanisms. You can either appeal to regulatory bodies for fair resolution or contact customer support. There is even an option to seek legal recourse, an option that is absent with illegal gambling sites. 

Legal operators provide tools players leverage to manage their gambling habits. When things go out of hand, they offer professional help. Above all, since illegal websites operate to steal your money, avoiding them minimizes such risk. Meanwhile, gambling on a licensed site guarantees you don’t break any law or suffer consequences. 

Common Types of Casino Scams 

Online casino scams exist in diff forms. Let us take a look at some common types you might encounter. 

  1. Fake online casinos: This is the most popular scam. The websites steal your personal information and money. They offer fake customer support. Likewise, misleading promotions of huge bonuses that don’t exist. 
  2. Rigged games: One of the ways legitimate gambling sites provide fairness is through Random Generated Numbers. Fake casinos tamper with the RGNs by misrepresenting them. As a result, players rarely win. 
  3. Payment scams: Rogue websites delay payment indefinitely or refuse to pay out winnings. More often than not, they cite unclear terms or a breach of the casino’s policies. Some operators grant bonuses with impossible wagering requirements to ensure players never cash out. 
  4. Identity theft, fraud, and scams: These websites steal a player’s information and use it for illegal activities. They sometimes obtain data via phishing scams. When players deposit money, the funds never appear in their betting account. The sites also deduct hidden fees or set up recurring charges on payment methods.  

Signs of an Online Casino Scam 

Scam operators are not invisible. Pay attention to the following telltale signs to detect rogue websites. 

  1. No registration or license information: Legitimate online casinos wear their license/registration information like a badge of honor. They display it on their homepage or create a dedicated “About Us” page for it. If you cannot find any clear information about their licensing, best avoid it. 
  2. Unrealistic promotion terms: If a bonus seems too good to be true, it probably is. Steer clear of gambling sites with unclear terms and conditions. One way to know is if the T&C is confusing. 
  3. Poor website design: Top-tier online casinos have well-designed websites with sitemaps for easy navigation. So, if the website you’re visiting appears unprofessional, outdated, or poorly designed, back off. Also, watch out for broken links. 
  4. Unknown software providers: Popular software providers publish information about certifications and software audits. Don’t play at an online casino that uses an unverified game provider. 
  5. Inadequate customer support: Customer support provides the first line of defense when something goes wrong. Therefore, avoid websites with no clear information on how to request help. Slow or unhelpful responses are also a telltale sign. 
  6. Suspicious deposit methods: Limited payment channels are a red flag. Some websites demand deposits via cryptocurrencies without providing an alternative. Since crypto transactions are untraceable, they run away with your money. 
  7. Pressure tactics: No well-meaning operator will request you make large deposits to claim time-bound bonuses and promotions. Similarly, they won’t send frequent unsolicited emails or employ aggressive marketing tactics. Any gambling website with no responsible gambling measures like self-exclusion and deposit limits is a scam. 

Steps to Take to Make Sure an Online Casino is Trustworthy 

Now that you know what a fake online casino looks like, explore the top ways to guarantee your security.

Check for a Valid License 

A valid license is primarily what differentiates a fake casino from a real one. The PAGCOR regulates, operates, and authorizes games of chance, cards, and numbers. In other words, a website without a permit is illegal. For foreign casinos, reputable licensing bodies include the UK Gambling Commission, Malta Gaming Authority, and Curacao. Check the bottom of the casino’s homepage to find the licensing information. 

Look for SSL Encryption 

SSL encryption refers to the standard technology for securing internet connection. It encrypts data between a website and a browser or two servers. As a result, the SSL prevents hackers from stealing information. When you visit a site’s URL, look for HTTPS and a padlock icon. A website with this feature offers a secure connection, and anything otherwise should be avoided. 

Research Reputation and Reviews 

The best way to know a casino’s performance compared to competitors is to read what past customers are saying about them. Therefore, take your time to browse independent reviews from reputable gaming websites. The experts use a unique methodology to evaluate an online casino from different perspectives. Factors they consider include security, payment method, mobile gaming, customer support, and available online casino games. Another way to research an operator’s reputation is to read negative and positive reviews in gambling forms to get a balanced perspective. 

Evaluate Payment Methods 

Investigate the deposit and withdrawal methods. Legit online casinos offer various secure payment options. They include credit/debit cards, e-wallets, and bank transfers. Likewise, mobile wallet services like GCash are available. GCash is the most popular in Filipino online casinos, so a site that does not offer it is likely fake. 

Read the Terms and Conditions 

Carefully read the terms and conditions. Pay attention to bonuses, wagering requirements, withdrawals. We recommend casinos with simple terms, as they won’t confuse you. 

Test Customer Support 

Choose a gambling site with multiple ways to contact support. The channels to look out for include phone numbers, live chat, email, and social media presence. Most importantly, test available support to note their efficiency. 

Verify Game Fairness 

Make sure the casino sources games from renowned software providers like Microgaming, Playtech, or Evolution Gaming. This way, you can ascertain if the games are audited for fairness by an independent testing agency like iTech Labs or eCOGRA. 

Check for Responsible Gambling Policies 

Check out responsible gaming tools offered by the casino. They include deposit limits, reality check questions, and self-exclusion options. The casino should also provide links to support organizations like GameCare or Gamblers Anonymous for problem gambling.

FAQs 

– Do Online Casinos Let You Win at First?

Winning in a legit casino requires luck and strategy. Any website that gives new players better odds to encourage continued gameplay is a scam, as it doesn’t operate under strict fair play regulations. 

– Do Online Casinos Pay Real Money?

Yes, a reputable online casino pays real money when you wager cash. That said, you won’t win money for playing demo games. 

– Do Online Casinos Track Your Winnings?

Gambling sites track winning for player account management. They also do so to comply with anti-money laundering laws, guarantee games are fair, and ensure responsible gambling.

– Can Online Slots Be Manipulated?
