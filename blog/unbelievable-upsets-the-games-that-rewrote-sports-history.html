More Shockwaves from Sports History

We all love a good upset story. One story that never seems to leave my brain is the night Buster Douglas knocked out a picture-perfect Mike Tyson in 1990. Las Vegas had given Douglas a ridiculous 42-to-1 line, but by Round 10, the shock felt like a live wire. Jump ahead to the 2015 Rugby World Cup and Japan, the obvious underdog again, stuns South Africa in a clash fans still call the Brighton Miracle. Neither shock was luck; history just loves to paint its biggest headlines in capital letters whenever the long shot wins.
