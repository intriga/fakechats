The Game of Cricket

Two teams, 11 players each – that is what you see when you take a look at a cricket game. One team bats, while the other bowls and fields. The batting team should score as many runs as possible, while the bowling team tries to dismiss the batsmen to limit the score.

The Types of Cricket

Do you know that the game of cricket has 3 different variations? We’ll talk about them below:

  • Test Cricket-5 Days: Old-fashioned and the most strategic type. It is played for 5 days with two innings per team. Each team bat twice in the match, and the result can be a draw or a win.
  • One-Day Internationals (50 Overs): Played over a single day, each team has 50 overs, 300 balls, to bat and bowl. This type of game features a faster pace than the first. So, you need to be ready for the more aggressive batting and strategic field placements.
  • T20 Cricket (20 Overs): The shortest version of the 3. Each team gets only 20 overs 120 balls. If you’re looking for the most entertaining style of play, you definitely should choose T20 matches.

Cricket Betting Markets

Are you aware of the different aspects you can bet on? Learn more below!

  • Match Winner: This is a bet on which team will win the match.
  • Top Batsman: Bet on the player who will score the most runs in the match or an innings.
  • Top Bowler: Predict which bowler will take the most wickets.
  • Total Runs (Over/Under): A bet on whether the total runs scored in a match will be over or under a given number.
  • Individual Player Performance: These bets are set based on specific milestones, including a player reaching a half-century or taking a certain number of wickets.

Understanding Form and Conditions

We encourage you to bet responsibly. We advise you not to rely on pure luck but to perform proper research. These are the main things you need to consider:

  • Team Form: A team’s recent performance, wins and losses, key injuries, and changes in the squad are the things that can turn over the results of the match. That’s why you need to grab as much information as you can before making your bet.
  • Pitch Conditions: The conditions of the pitch need to be looked at right after you research the team’s form. There are a few ticks: a dry pitch, like the ones in India, offers the best conditions for spinners. Wet or grassy ones, like in Australia, could assist fast bowlers. 
  • Weather Conditions: Weather is an important factor, especially in limited-overs formats like One Day Internationals (ODIs) and T20s. Cloudy weather can be a big help to the bowlers in the game, while rain can even reduce the number of overs.

The Betting Strategies and Tips

There is a high chance you’re betting on a live match, so here’s how to win cricket betting.

  • Familiarise Yourself with the Terms: This is a no-brainer, but you definitely need to learn the game-specific terms.
  • Observe the Momentum: In-play betting gives you a great chance to pounce on a change in momentum. If the main batter falls or if the bowling attack of a team is running riot, the odds fluctuate very fast. A bet during this period could result in fabulous returns.
  • Bet on Partnerships and Milestones: The game of cricket is about partnerships, and correct estimation of these partnerships means getting good returns. Partnerships make all the difference in gathering momentum toward a big score.
  • Bet Small, Bet Smart: Never overextend on in-play bets. Smaller bets based on live insight into the match, e.g. momentum shifts and player performance, are better.
  • Observe the First Few Overs: We always recommend waiting for at least a few overs so that you can get an idea about the pitch conditions, how both teams are playing, and how their strategies are unfolding.

How to Understand Odds Movements 

Identifying the perfect timing of a bet involves tracking odds movements. The odds will, of course, change depending on the team’s performance, player injuries, or any important moments that happened during the match. It can be more profitable to place early bets as they give you better odds. However, it is not that simple. During the match, you can get a feel for the situation on the field and make a smarter bet, which will ultimately bring you more profit.

But what else is the difference between these two types of bets?

  • Future bets: Bets that include the outcome of a series or tournament. For example, in the World Cup, you need to predict which team will win. We recommend doing comprehensive research, starting with long-term trends.
  • Value bets: With this type of bet, the odds are higher than your idea of ​​the actual probability of an event.

Another important tip is to stick to the rules of responsible gambling. Remember the importance of bankroll management, never bet on emotions, and use research and logic when placing bets. If you feel that you are unable to cope on your own, seek help in the “Responsible Gaming” section, which is available on any official betting site.
